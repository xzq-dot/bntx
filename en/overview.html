03 The PD-(L)1 × VEGF Bispecific Race

◆ Lung-cancer leader

Akeso + Summit

ivonescimab · PD-1×VEGF

The only drug with head-to-head data beating Keytruda — cut progression/death risk by 50% in NSCLC, igniting the whole field.

◆ Differentiated play

BioNTech + BMS

BNT327 · PD-L1×VEGF

Binds ligand PD-L1 rather than the receptor. Avoids the lung head-on fight; targets breast (ORR 73.8%), colorectal, liver, SCLC (85% response).

◆ Fast followers

Merck & others

multiple · via BD deals

Buying in through partnerships. The field still needs Phase 3 to confirm survival benefit — some already question if it's the wrong target.

05 Risk / Reward Verdict

▲ Bull case
  • €17.2B cash = no survival risk even after years of losses
  • Strong early BNT327 data + BMS's huge backing
  • Oncology's ceiling and margins far exceed vaccines
  • Stock already at range bottom; bad news priced in
▼ Bear case
  • Losses expected for 3+ years, no positive cash flow
  • Valuation hinges on one pipeline's binary outcome
  • The PD-1/VEGF field has had recent clinical failures
  • Leadership transition + execution risk
In a sentence: BNTX is essentially an "option-like" biotech — a thick cash cushion below, oncology upside above, and years of losses plus a binary bet in between. Suited to investors who can stomach high volatility and wait 3–5 years for BNT327 to deliver; not for those seeking steady income. Watch one signal above all: BNT327's Phase 3 data.
