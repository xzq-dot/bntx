Drug Profile · BioNTech's Core Oncology Asset

BNT327 pumitamig · BMS-986545 · formerly PM8002

A PD-L1 × VEGF-A bispecific antibody — the lead character of BioNTech's whole oncology turnaround. Nearly all of the valuation's upside rides on this one drug.

01 Dual Mechanism of Action

◆ PD-L1 checkpoint inhibition

Release the immune "brake"

Binds PD-L1 on tumor cells, restoring T cells' ability to recognize and kill cancer — the action of classic PD-1/PD-L1 immunotherapy.

+
◆ VEGF-A neutralization

Cut off the tumor's supply

Neutralizes VEGF-A to sever the new blood vessels feeding the tumor (anti-angiogenesis), while reversing the immunosuppressive microenvironment.

Key differentiation: it binds the ligand PD-L1 (on tumors) rather than the PD-1 receptor (on T cells), aiming to localize anti-VEGF activity within the tumor microenvironment for greater precision and less systemic exposure — the main mechanistic difference from rival ivonescimab (which binds PD-1).

05 Risk Note

⚠ Strong early response ≠ eventual approval

What Phase 3 really tests is long-term overall survival (OS) and safety; a striking early objective response rate (ORR) does not necessarily translate into approval. The PD-1/VEGF field has had recent trials fall short of expectations. Because of the 50/50 split, BioNTech is deeply tied to this drug — success means a re-rate, failure cuts deep. BNT327's Phase 3 top-line data is the single decisive variable for the BNTX share price.
